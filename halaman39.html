🩺 Screening Section Dr. Meow

Important for several main reasons:

  • ✅ 1. Identify Your Cat’s Current Health Condition
    🐱 Screening allows the vet to detect early signs of illness, abnormal behavior, or hidden symptoms that owners may not notice.
    💉 This is important even when visiting only for vaccination or grooming.
  • ✅ 2. Prevent Infection or Risk to Other Cats
    🤧 If there are symptoms such as sneezing, watery eyes, or weakness, your cat might pose a risk to others in the clinic.
    🚨 Screening enables isolation or immediate action if necessary.
  • ✅ 3. Early Preparation for Treatment
    🩻 The vet can determine if further procedures (like blood tests, X-rays, or specific medication) are needed.
    🕒 It also helps the vet schedule time and prioritize cases based on true health needs.
  • ✅ 4. Pre-Procedural Evaluation
    🐾 If your cat is coming for neutering, vaccination, or boarding — early screening ensures it’s healthy and safe for the procedure.
  • ✅ 5. Reducing Risk of Complications
    🩺 Basic examination can detect early problems that could worsen if untreated — such as high fever, wounds, dehydration, or anemia.

✨ In short:
Screening isn’t just a formality. It’s an essential step to protect your cat’s health — and other cats in the clinic too.
